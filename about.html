About Me

I’m Panagiotis Karagiannis, a passionate Software Engineer with a deep love for robotics and motorsport technology. Since July 2024, I’ve been part of Manthey Racing’s Vehicle Science Department, where I continue to push the boundaries of automotive engineering.

Before joining Manthey Racing, I was a key contributor to the FaSTTUBe Formula Student team, where I led the development of their first autonomous vehicle system. This marked FaSTTUBe’s debut in the driverless event category, and through my efforts, we significantly advanced autonomous racing technologies. My work encompassed the entire autonomous driving pipeline, from low-level controls and state estimation to vehicle control and path planning.

My journey in software engineering started with a strong academic foundation at the Technical University of Berlin, where I completed both my Bachelor's and Master's in Computer Science. During this time, I also worked as a Research Assistant in the Machine Learning Department, helping integrate innovative tools such as an autograding framework for coursework.

I’m a major proponent of open-source software and strive to make my personal projects openly available to the community. During my time at FaSTTUBe, I open-sourced the path planning algorithm I developed, which has since been adopted by many teams worldwide. My approach emphasizes precision, efficiency, and robust architecture, ensuring that every project I contribute to meets the highest standards. Whether it's developing advanced control systems or working on user-friendly tools, I always aim to create impactful, reliable, and accessible software.

If you’re interested in autonomous systems, motorsports technology, or anything in between, feel free to connect with me!

Career Milestones

  • Software Engineer at Manthey Racing since July 2024, working in the Vehicle Science Department.
  • Led the development of FaSTTUBe’s first autonomous vehicle system, contributing to multiple podium placements in Formula Student competitions.
  • Developed a cutting-edge machine learning vehicle control system for my Master's thesis, enhancing autonomous racing capabilities.
  • Created and maintained an innovative open-source path planning algorithm, widely adopted by Formula Student teams globally.
  • Presented at the Autonomous Racing Workshop (ARWo) and awarded Best Presentation among entries from over 40 teams.
  • Developed a tool to convert hand-drawn Formula Student track layouts into valid simulation tracks, streamlining testing for FaSTTUBe.
  • Integrated autonomous driving algorithms with the Live for Speed simulation environment, enabling cost-effective testing for autonomous systems.
  • Research Assistant in Machine Learning at the Technical University of Berlin, improving course management with an autograding framework.
